Ti/Tv Ratio
Transition / Transversion ratio for SNPs only (MNPs excluded).
Random DNA ≈ 0.5. Functional sites show 2–3 due to purifying selection. Values >2 here suggest enrichment of constrained sites within candidate loci.

Subgenome Bias
(C − E) / (C + E). Range: −1 to +1.
+1 = all variants on C subgenome. −1 = all on E. ~0 = balanced. Strong positive values expected for SH3 and lr (documented C-subgenome QTLs).

HC Fraction
% of variants in Tier 1 (QTL-constrained chromosomal windows).
Values near 1.0 mean most hits fall inside the published QTL interval — strongly validating the annotation. Low values indicate mostly serendipitous Discovery-tier hits.

Density / Mbp
Annotated variants per megabase of candidate region.
Normalises for QTL window size — allows fair comparison between e.g. SH3 (13 Mb window) and lr (whole chromosome). High density = locally diverse or structurally variable locus.

Clustered Variants
Variants within 1 kb of at least one other variant.
Physically clustered variants may represent: a complex allele called as multiple simple events, a gene under recent selection pressure, or structural variation in the allotetraploid genome. Expected to be high in NBS-LRR clusters.

Gene Hit Rate
Unique genes hit / total candidate genes indexed.
Low values (<0.1) = sparse coverage, most candidate genes have no variant in this dataset. High values = broad polymorphism across the locus. Proxy for VCF completeness relative to locus size.

Variant Type Distribution
SNPs: 1-bp substitutions. Insertions/Deletions: length-changing variants. MNPs: multi-nucleotide substitutions of equal length — classified separately from SNPs and excluded from Ti/Tv.
